Four activities to do in Cantabria

Cueva de Covalanas

The Covalanas cave, the cave with red hind, lies on the northeast slope of Monte Pando, over the El Mirón cavity, used as a habitation at least for the last 45,000 years.

Capricho de Gaudí

El Capricho is a villa in designed by Antoni Gaudí. It was built in 1883-1885 for the summer use of a wealthy client, Máximo Díaz de Quijano, who died a year before the house was completed.

Playa de Sonabia

About 14km east of Laredo, small, wild Playa de Sonabia is tucked into a rock-lined inlet beneath high crags, above which rare griffon vultures circle the sky.

Hiking through Picos de Europa

The Picos de Europa ("Peaks of Europe", also the Picos) are a mountain range extending for about 20 km (12 mi), forming part of the Cantabrian Mountains in northern Spain. It is Spain's only inhabited nature reserve, spread over 11 villages.
